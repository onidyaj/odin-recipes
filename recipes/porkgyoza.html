Pork Gyoza Recipe

Description

Gyoza are Japanese pan-fried dumplings that can be stuffed with numerous combinations of meats and vegetables. They make the perfect savory appetizer to any meal

Ingredients

  • 2 cups ground pork
  • 2 medium mushrooms, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, chopped
  • 1 tbsp rice wine
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp ginger, grated
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • pinch of salt
  • Dumpling wrappers

Directions

  1. Combine pork, mushrooms, garlic, rice wine, soy sauce, ginger, sesame oil, and salt in a large bowl. Mix well until it forms a uniform texture.
  2. Take one round dumpling wrapper and put 2 tbsp of the pork mixture on one side of the wrapper, leaving room at the edges. Brush a thin coat of water along the open space. Fold the wrapper over itself into a semi-circle shape and firmly press down on the edges. Use a fork to crimp the edges. Repeate for remainining gyoza.
  3. Heat oil in large pan over medium heat. Place the gyoza into the pan in one layer. Allow to cook for 3-4 minutes.
  4. Add 4-6 tbsp of water into the pan and cover with a lid. Allow the steam to cook the rest of the gyoza for 3-4 minutes or until the water has evaporated and the meat has cooked.
  5. Serve by placing a plate upside down over the gyoza, and flipping the entire pan upside down so the plate is right side up. The gyoza should be served brown-side up.
